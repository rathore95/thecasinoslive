Need Help?

If you or someone you know has a gambling problem, seek help immediately:

  • BeGambleAware - UK-based gambling support
  • GamCare - Free support and treatment
  • National Council on Problem Gambling (NCPG) - US-based help
  • Gamblers Anonymous - Support groups worldwide
  • ConnexOntario - Free, confidential support for problem gambling in Canada

Gambling Should Be Entertainment, Not Income

Gambling should be fun and entertaining. It should never be considered a way to make money or solve financial problems.

Set Limits

  • Budget Limits: Only gamble with money you can afford to lose
  • Time Limits: Set a time limit and stick to it
  • Loss Limits: Decide your maximum loss before you start
  • Never Chase Losses: Accept losses and move on

Warning Signs of Problem Gambling

  • Spending more money or time gambling than you can afford
  • Lying to family or friends about gambling
  • Feeling guilty or anxious about gambling
  • Borrowing money to gamble
  • Neglecting work, family, or personal responsibilities
  • Chasing losses or gambling to win back money

Self-Exclusion Programs

Most licensed online casinos offer self-exclusion programs that allow you to temporarily or permanently block access to gambling services. If you feel you need a break, use these tools.

18+ Only

You must be at least 18 years old to gamble. It is illegal for minors to gamble in most jurisdictions.

Get Help

If you think you have a gambling problem, don't wait. Reach out to one of the organizations listed above for free, confidential support.
